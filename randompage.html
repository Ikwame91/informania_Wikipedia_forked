The Theory of Relativity, proposed by Albert Einstein, revolutionized our understanding of space, time, and gravity. It consists of two main theories: Special Relativity and General Relativity.

Special Relativity, published in 1905, introduced the concept that the laws of physics are the same for all non-accelerating observers and that the speed of light in a vacuum is constant for all observers, regardless of their relative motion. General Relativity, published in 1915, explains gravity as a curvature of spacetime caused by mass and energy. It predicts phenomena such as the bending of light around massive objects and the existence of black holes.

The Theory of Relativity has been confirmed by numerous experiments and observations and continues to be a cornerstone of modern physics.

Did you know?

The Amazon Rainforest, located in South America, is the largest tropical rainforest in the world, covering an area of over 6.7 million square kilometers (2.7 million square miles). It is home to an incredibly diverse array of plant and animal species.

Did you also know that the Amazon Rainforest produces 20% of the world's oxygen and plays a crucial role in regulating the Earth's climate?

On this Day

February 25th is the anniversary of the discovery of penicillin by Scottish biologist Alexander Fleming in 1928. Penicillin, the first antibiotic drug, revolutionized medicine and has saved millions of lives by effectively treating bacterial infections.

On this day in history, in 1986, Ferdinand Marcos, the President of the Philippines, fled the country following the People Power Revolution, ending his 20-year authoritarian rule. The revolution marked a triumph of democracy and led to the restoration of democratic institutions in the Philippines.
